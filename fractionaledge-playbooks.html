Playbook 1: Product-Market Fit Framework

The biggest challenge for experienced professionals is translating "what they're good at" into an offering that markets itself. This framework helps identify the intersection of client pain, market demand, and your expertise.

Target Customer
Who has the problem?
Underserved Needs
What keeps them awake at night?
Value Proposition
How do you solve it uniquely?
Service Feature Set
What exactly do you deliver?
Client Experience
How does the engagement feel?

The 5-Question Fit Test

Run every potential service offering through this test:

Fit Question 1: Who specifically has this problem?

Bad answer: "Companies that need help with finance."

Good answer: "Series A-B SaaS founders who just raised their first significant round and need to build a financial foundation without hiring a full-time CFO."

Key indicators: Industry + Stage + Role + Problem = Very specific description

Fit Question 2: How urgent is the problem?

  • High urgency: They're losing money, missing deadlines, facing a deadline
  • Medium urgency: They're inefficient, frustrated, know they should act
  • Low urgency: "Nice to have" - these are slow sales, price-sensitive

Fit Question 3: What's their alternative today?

Understanding alternatives reveals your real competition:

  • Do nothing (most common)
  • Hire full-time (expensive, may not fit)
  • Use existing staff (overwhelmed, unqualified)
  • Cheap freelancer (quality issues)
  • Competitor firm (you need differentiation)

Fit Question 4: Can they articulate the problem clearly?

If prospects can name the problem and its cost, they're further along in their journey. Look for:

  • They use specific terminology
  • They know what they want the outcome to look like
  • They can describe the cost of inaction

Fit Question 5: What's the ROI reality?

Calculate the value-to-price ratio:

  • Clear ROI: "Spend $5K, save/make $50K" - easy sell
  • Soft ROI: "Spend $5K, feel more confident" - harder sell
  • Risk reversal needed: Offer pilots, guarantees

The 30-Second Fit Statement

Use this formula to quickly test and communicate product-market fit:

Formula: "I help [specific client] achieve [specific outcome] by [your method], even when [common objection/alternative]." Example: "I help Series A SaaS CFOs close their books 3x faster by implementing automated financial processes, even when they think they're too small for that kind of infrastructure."

Playbook 2: Client Acquisition System

The #1 mistake fractional executives make: jumping straight to tactics (networking events, LinkedIn outreach) without building a foundation. This playbook creates a systematic, predictable client acquisition engine.

The Three-Channel Acquisition Model

01

Inbound

Prospects find you through content, search, or referrals. Lower effort per lead, but requires consistent content creation and takes time to build momentum.

  • LinkedIn thought leadership
  • Blog posts answering FAQs
  • Speaking at events
  • Referral partnerships
02

Warm Outbound

You reach out to prospects where you have a connection or introduction. Higher response rates than cold outreach, requires relationship building first.

  • Reconnect with former colleagues
  • Ask for introductions from warm network
  • Engage with content prospects read
  • Strategic partnership referrals
03

Pilot Projects

Low-risk first engagements that prove value. Turns prospects into clients and clients into references. The easiest sale because risk is minimized.

  • Diagnostic assessments
  • Focused project scopes
  • 30-60 day pilots
  • Quick wins with defined outcomes

12-Week Acquisition Sprint

Weeks 1-2: Foundation

Define your ICP precisely. Rewrite LinkedIn headline and summary. Create one-line value proposition. Update website to reflect niche positioning.

Weeks 3-4: Content System

Choose 2 content channels maximum (LinkedIn + one other). Plan 4 weeks of problem-focused content. Start publishing consistently. Focus on problems, not you.

Weeks 5-6: Warm Network Outreach

Reach out to 5-10 former colleagues per week. Send value-first messages, not sales pitches. Ask for intros to their network. Track every conversation.

Weeks 7-8: Funnel Development

Design your diagnostic offering. Create simple landing page. Write automated follow-up sequences. Test your sales process with 3-5 prospects.

Weeks 9-10: Pilot Launch

Offer first pilot project to warm leads. Deliver exceptional value. Document results. Ask for testimonials and referrals immediately.

Weeks 11-12: Refine & Scale

Analyze what's working. Double down on winning channels. Refine messaging based on feedback. Build pipeline for next quarter.

Outreach Cadence Template

Day 1 - LinkedIn Connection Request: "[Name], I've been following your work at [Company] and would love to connect. I help [ICP] achieve [outcome] and I'd value the opportunity to learn more about what you're building." Day 3 - LinkedIn Message (after connection accepted): "Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Day 5 - Email (to same person): Subject: Quick question about [Company]'s [specific initiative] Hi [Name], I came across [recent news about Company] and was impressed by [specific detail]. Playbook 3: Discovery Process & Questionnaire

The discovery process determines whether both parties are a good fit. The goal is to understand deeply, position appropriately, and set up a clear next step.

The 5-Phase Discovery Framework

0-2 min
Open & Set Agenda

Build rapport, confirm purpose

2-12 min
Situation Assessment

Understand their world

12-25 min
Pain Discovery

Uncover real challenges

25-28 min
Qualify & Position

Is fit + value clear?

28-30 min
Next Steps

Clear call to action

Discovery Questionnaire

Section 1: Opening & Rapport (2 min)

"Before we dive in, can you share a bit about your background and how you ended up at [Company]?"

"What's on your agenda for this conversation? What would make this a great use of our time together?"

Section 2: Situation Assessment (10 min)

"Tell me about [Company] - where are you in your journey, and what's the current state of [relevant department/function]?"

"What does a typical day or week look like for you right now? Where does your time go?"

"Who else is involved in [area of concern], and how do decisions typically get made?"

Section 3: Pain Discovery (13 min)

"What prompted you to explore solutions like this now? What changed?"

"On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate how [specific area] is working today? What would get you to a 9 or 10?"

"What's the cost of this challenge - not just financially, but in terms of time, stress, or missed opportunities?"

"What have you tried before to address this? What worked, and what fell short?"

"What does success look like for this initiative? What specific outcomes are you hoping for?"

Section 4: Qualification (3 min)

"Do you have a budget range in mind for this, and what's the timeline you're working toward?"

"Who else would need to be convinced for this to move forward?"

Section 5: Position & Next Steps (2 min)

After gathering this information, summarize what you heard and position your value:

"What I'm hearing is [summarize key points]. Based on what you've shared, I believe we could help by [specific approach]. Here's what I'd suggest as next steps..."

Discovery Scoring: Is This a Good Fit?

Red Flags (Caution)

  • No clear pain - just "exploring"
  • Budget significantly below market rate
  • Too many stakeholders with competing agendas
  • Timeline doesn't align with solution delivery
  • Previous bad experiences with similar help
  • Culture clash or value misalignment

Green Lights (Strong Fit)

  • Clear, urgent pain they can articulate
  • Budget is reasonable and allocated
  • Single decision-maker or aligned team
  • Timeline allows for proper execution
  • History of investing in help
  • Culture and values alignment
